Neurochemical Mapping
9 Reward Pathway Predictions
Drop-Reset
Phasic dopamine craving–hit–baseline loop. No resolution. Addiction cycling analog.
Continuous Ascent
Delayed gratification. Sustained anticipation with full resolution. Healthy goal-directed behavior.
Intimate Narrative
Attachment circuitry. Oxytocin–dopamine overlap. Emotional salience over sonic climax.
Call-Response
Social reward circuitry. Partial reward sustaining anticipation. Interpersonal dialogue dopamine.
Burst / Compressed
Impulsivity analog. Immediate release, no anticipation phase. Distinct from cycling — no craving buildup.
Perpetual Groove
Motor-dopamine coupling. Basal ganglia rhythm entrainment. Synchrony reward. Lateral, not phasic.
Plateau / Sustained
Tonic dopamine. Flow-state analog. Sustained engagement without cycling. Possibly meditative.
Gospel / Communal
Communal vocalization endorphins. Oxytocin via group synchrony. Emotional catharsis. Escalation without conventional resolution — transcendence.
Psychedelic / Improv
Novelty-seeking dopamine (reward of the unexpected). Serotonergic entrainment in intended chemical context. Architecture explicitly designed for altered neurochemical states — unique in the dataset.

Clinical Parallel — Secondary Analysis
Country's architectural migration from Type 3 to Type 1 occurred between 2008–2016. The opioid epidemic in rural white America accelerated between 2010–2017. Geographic, demographic, temporal, and now architectural — the most clinically pointed secondary analysis the study can produce.

Folklore, Evermore — voluntary retreat from Type 1 at peak commercial success; unique in the dataset
No other artist of comparable commercial scale has voluntarily abandoned Type 1 architecture and reversed to patient earned-resolution. Country music made the same migration but has not reversed. Swift's arc is the contemporary dataset's most important single-artist data point — same window, same destination, different outcome.
Type 9 — Architecture Predicts Substance
The Grateful Dead's audience substance use was predominantly psychedelic rather than opioid or stimulant. If the study demonstrates that drop-reset cycling correlates with opioid/stimulant use and psychedelic architecture correlates with psychedelic use, the architecture-predicts-substance hypothesis is supported across two data points simultaneously. Type 9 makes that test possible. The Dead's crowd was also a structural participant in the architecture — the only case in the dataset where the audience co-generates the form in real time.

Gospel — Secular Derivatives
Jackie WilsonEarliest bridge — pure gospel vocal architecture (melisma, escalation, breaking point) applied to secular R&B; influenced James Brown and Michael Jackson; makes downstream lineage traceable
Sam CookeGospel architecture translated intact into secular pop — same escalation mechanics in You Send Me as in Soul Stirrers recordings; clearest proof the taxonomy is structural not genre-based
Otis ReddingTry a Little Tenderness: opens as Type 3, transforms into Type 8 within a single track — within-song architectural migration and proof of concept for Spotify segment-level methodology
Al GreenReverse migration: secular Type 3/4 → ordained minister → pure Type 8; same voice traversing two architecture types across a documented biographical arc — rarest evidence in the dataset
Ray CharlesWhat'd I Say — gospel call-response enters the secular Billboard corpus; bridges Types 4 and 8 in the public record
Bill WithersNo gospel background — arrived at Type 8 architecture through emotional instinct alone. Lean on Me: verse as solo testimony, chorus as collective invitation, bridge as direct request for participation. Lovely Day: longest sustained note in pop recording history — the gospel transcendence moment, frozen and extended.

Cross-Taxonomy Case Study — The Beatles (Six of Nine Architecture Types)
Type 3 (Yesterday, Blackbird — intimate narrative) · Type 2 (A Day in the Life — ascent sections) · Type 4 (I've Got a Feeling — call-response) · Type 5 (Helter Skelter — burst) · Type 8 (Let It Be — gospel-adjacent) · Type 9 (Tomorrow Never Knows, I Am the Walrus, Revolution 9 — psychedelic/improvisational). Their structural evolution from 1962 to 1970 traces six of the nine architecture types and makes them the single most valuable cross-taxonomy validation case in the dataset. The reclassification of Tomorrow Never Knows from Type 7 (plateau) to Type 9 (psychedelic) is supported by the deliberate disorientation and tape-loop construction — sustained calm vs. intended neurochemical disruption are architecturally distinct.
